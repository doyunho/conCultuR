# Interpretation guide

## Correct interpretation

- A high attention index means high digital visibility in the selected platforms and time window.
- A high attention gap means low visibility relative to conservation-status rank.
- High platform disagreement means that the selected platforms do not tell the same attention story.
- High query sensitivity means that the result depends strongly on query wording.
- Campaign effects are best interpreted as campaign-associated deviations unless the design supports causal inference.

## Incorrect interpretation

Do not interpret outputs as:

- species abundance
- population trend
- extinction risk
- ecological importance
- formal conservation priority
- public support for conservation action

## Recommended language

Use:

> We identified potential communication-priority gaps.

Avoid:

> We identified the species most in need of conservation.

Use:

> Attention increased relative to a fitted counterfactual after the campaign date.

Avoid:

> The campaign caused public attention to increase.

## Minimum reporting checklist

- concept dictionary
- query audit
- platform coverage
- standardisation method
- attention index date range
- attention-gap thresholds
- threshold sensitivity
- campaign window and placebo design where relevant
- interpretation caveat distinguishing attention from ecological value
